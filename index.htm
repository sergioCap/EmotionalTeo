NEURODEVELOPMENTAL DISORDERS AND ROBOTS
Neurodevelopmental disorders are impairments that concern the development of the central nervous system. One of the most diffused, Autism, has to do with the social interaction and the communication, and can be more or less noticeable, depending on the subject and on the level it affects him.

Experts conducted studies on people affected by autism and they found out that a possible way to help them in improving their cognitive skills is represented by motion-based interaction. This seems to be more attracting to autistic people, than interfacing directly with therapists.

For this reasons, the use of robots in therapy is spreading, in particular for engaging and entertaining activities. This may shorten the distance between the real world and the autistic person.

Teo2 is the new version of Teo. It has been developed by Stefano Tolomeo as master thesis at Politecnico di Milano.

The main features that make Teo2 different from Teo are:
  • The face: Teo2 has a programmable LED matrix as face. Thanks to this, the facial expressions of the robot are highly customizable, unlike the ones of Teo’s face.
  • The body: Teo2’s body has been equipped with five FSR sensors, which can be used to sense and recognize touch interaction.
  • Sensors: Teo2’s body and base have been equipped with several new sensors like gyroscope, IR sensors, PIR sensors, Sonar, and others.

These new features allow Teo2 to be able to recognize and express emotions and feelings and for this reason Teo2 has also been called Emotional Teo.

TEO - The quest of the Princess Mafalda
Teo and the child interact through a story, taking them in a completely immersive adventure. The interaction is based on all the possible multisensorial stimuli and the child participates and is attracted by sound effects, music, storytelling and active participation.

The child is the main character of the story and has to accomplish a mission, getting rid of all the difficulties and participating in a walkthrough quest.

The story takes place a long time ago, in a charming place, where a naughty dragon has kidnapped a beautiful princess of the village. Her father, the King, prise the bravest people to go find his daughter and free her from the dragon. Whoever reaches them and saves the princess will become knight of the kingdom.

Teo is the guide of the activity, he motivates the child and also helps him in finding objects necessary to complete the story.

The user experience is based on different elements, technologies and instruments.
In particular, we use:
  • vocal recordings;
  • physical objects;
  • multimedia contents;
  • facial expressions;
  • touch sensing;
  • physical buttons;
  • movements.
The combination of those enriches the full experience and makes it more engaging. Rewarding is also used and is probably the thing that involves the child the most. In fact, every time a goal has been reached, Teo cheers and at the end of the story multimedia content is displayed to congratulate with the child.

TALK2TEO
For this scenario, the context of use is set in the therapy sessions were the therapist guide the child through a set of activities specifically designed to help him practice some skills.

As part of the development of these activities, Teo2 is to be involved as a companion for the child during the process and, as a tool for the therapist, who should be able to control Teo and use it to provide positive reinforcement through mood changes, vocal praisals and movements that can be related to a Teo's emotion.

Moreover, Teo is able to react to the external stimuli so to unfold behaviors that the children can learn to relate to some cause and that should also provide the feeling that Teo is "alive".

The therapist controls Teo using the joypad configured before, allowing a free to play approach. The behaviours can be divided in manual and automatic. The first ones are those controllable through the joypad used by the therapist and the second ones are the autonomous responses of Teo that are result of different kind of interaction and external stimuli.

Manual behaviours:
  1. Controlled Move/Rotate
  2. Pre-de ned phrases triggering: allows to play phrases such as "Do you want to play?", "Hello!", "Great!", "Well done!", etc.
  3. Mood selection/triggering (happy, sad, angry, scared). Everytime a mood is selected, an animation follows. All of the animation change the displayed face is changed and a consisting sound with a voice pitch depending on the mood is played, followed by the movement described bellow.
    • Happy: Teo rotates 30° clockwise, 60° counter-clockwise and 30° clockwise.
    • Sad: Teo rotates 180° clockwise
    • Scared: Teo goes back 40cm.
    • Angry: Teo moves forward 20cm.
  4. Non-linguistic utterances
    • high pro le: laugh, cry
    • low pro le: nod, gasp, sigh
  5. Idle state selection: in this state Teo simulates to be sleeping.

Automatic behaviours:
  1. Face animation
    2. Every three seconds a face animation, of the current selected mood, occurs.
  2. Breathing
    3. A fading e ect on the led strips simulates the breathing with a frequence that depends on the mood.
  3. Wake up
    4. In the idle state, Teo is sleeping and snoring. Teo has to be awakened by the child which has to talk to it or can be awaken by the therapist through the joypad. Once woken up, Teo says Hello!
  4. Hit
    5. When child hits Teo, it gets sad and cries.
  5. Where are you?
    6. When the child goes away from Teo, that is, when the child is not perceived close to Teo closer than the personal zone (less than 2m), Teo gets sad saying "Where are you?". It rotates 60° both to the left and to the right, as if it were looking for the child. When the child comes back (i.e. comes back to the personal zone), Teo turns happy saying "Do you want to play?".
  6. Prompts
    7. When Teo has a random time between 30 to 50 seconds of inactivity (no other behavior, either manual or automatic, has been executed), Teo makes a low profile non-linguistic utterance or a random phrase and rotates 30°, both to the left and to the right. This with the intention to keep the robot active and trying to engage the children in some kind of interaction.
MARCOPOLO
Marcopolo is a simple game that consists of the following: among a group of people one is selected to be blindfolded and whose objective would be to catch someone else.

In order to do that, the only cue that can use to guide the blindfolded person is based on a call, made by the searcher, saying "Marco!" to which the others respond "Polo!".
Thus, based on the sound the blindfolded person must move around following the responses until he catches someone. The call can be used as many time as it is needed for the game to finish.

This scenario is based on this game. In this way, Teo can be used also to develop games that allow the children to get used to interact with Teo while exploiting the nature of the interaction to incorporate tasks that target the development and improvement of some skills that can be useful.
